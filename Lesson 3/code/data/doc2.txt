Cybersecurity Training
The Cybersecurity Training Policy for Smart Energy Grids
Purpose and Scope
The purpose of this policy is to enhance cybersecurity awareness and competence across all 
organizational levels by providing structured training programs tailored to the unique 
challenges of securing smart energy grids. These programs aim to reduce human error, 
address emerging threats, and ensure compliance with relevant regulations, including the 
NIS2 Directive.
The scope of this policy encompasses all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors 
with access to organizational systems, data, or facilities, emphasizing their critical role in 
maintaining cybersecurity and operational integrity.
Training Objectives
The objectives of the cybersecurity training program are as follows:
- Increase Awareness of Common Cybersecurity Threats: Educate employees, contractors, 
and vendors about common threats such as phishing, malware, and ransomware, and 
provide best practices for mitigating these risks.
- Ensure Compliance with Regulatory Requirements: Ensure that all personnel are familiar 
with relevant cybersecurity regulations, such as the NIS2 Directive, Article 21, Clause 2(g).
- Build Technical and Non-Technical Capabilities: Develop both technical and non-technical 
staff skills to prevent, detect, and respond to cybersecurity incidents, with a focus on smart 
grid infrastructure and its critical role in energy operations.
Training Content
Core topics covered in the training program:
- Recognizing and Reporting Phishing Attempts and Social Engineering Attacks
Employees and contractors will learn how to identify common tactics used in phishing 
emails, phone scams, and other forms of social engineering, and understand the process for 
reporting suspicious activity.
- Secure Password Practices and Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
Training includes best practices for creating and managing strong passwords, the 
importance of avoiding password reuse, and the implementation of MFA to strengthen 
access controls.
- Identifying and Responding to Cybersecurity Incidents
Personnel will be trained to detect signs of a potential breach, understand immediate 
response protocols, and ensure timely escalation to the appropriate teams.
- Best Practices for Handling Sensitive Data and Preventing Data Breaches
Training focuses on safeguarding sensitive organizational information through proper data 
classification, encryption, and secure sharing methods.
- Awareness of Advanced Threats
Participants will gain insights into emerging cybersecurity challenges such as ransomware 
attacks and insider threats, along with strategies to mitigate these risks effectively.
Frequency of Training
- Annual Training: All employees, contractors, and third-party vendors must complete 
mandatory cybersecurity training once per year. This ensures consistent awareness of key 
principles and compliance with organizational policies and regulatory requirements, 
including the NIS2 Directive.
- Onboarding Sessions: New hires and contractors are required to participate in 
cybersecurity training within their first month of joining. These sessions provide 
foundational knowledge about the organization's security protocols and expectations.
- Ad-hoc Sessions: Additional training sessions are organized as needed in response to 
significant cybersecurity incidents, regulatory updates, or changes in organizational 
security practices.
Mandatory Participation
Participation in cybersecurity training is a mandatory requirement for all personnel, 
including employees, third-party contractors, and vendors who access the organization’s 
systems, data, or facilities. This ensures that all individuals are adequately prepared to 
recognize and mitigate cybersecurity risks, contributing to the overall resilience of the 
organization's operations.
Consequences of Non-Compliance:
* Restricted System Access: Personnel who do not complete training may lose access to 
critical systems and resources until the training is completed.
* Disciplinary Actions: For employees, repeated non-compliance may lead to disciplinary 
measures in accordance with the organization’s policies.
* Contractual Repercussions: For vendors and contractors, non-compliance could affect 
their contractual status or eligibility for future engagements.
Assessment and Testing
Each cybersecurity training session is designed to not only deliver critical information but 
also assess the effectiveness of the training and ensure practical application of the concepts. 
By incorporating interactive and evaluative components, RGN4 aims to enhance knowledge 
retention and readiness.
Components of Training Sessions:
* Quizzes for Knowledge Retention: Short quizzes at the end of each session evaluate 
participants’ understanding of core topics.
* Simulated Phishing Exercises: Regular simulations test participants' ability to recognize 
and respond to phishing attempts. Results are anonymized but used for improvement 
strategies and additional training if necessary.
* Role-Specific Case Studies: Case studies tailored to specific roles help participants apply 
training content to real-world scenarios.
Tailored Training Programs
The cybersecurity training program is designed to address the unique needs and 
responsibilities of various organizational roles within RGN4. By tailoring content to specific 
groups, the organization ensures that all personnel possess the knowledge and skills 
necessary to protect critical systems and data.
Role-Specific Training Tracks:
* Technical Staff:
+ Focus Areas: Advanced understanding of system vulnerabilities and secure 
configurations.
+ Methods: Interactive workshops, penetration testing exercises, and live incident 
simulations.
* Senior Management:
+ Focus Areas: Strategic oversight of cybersecurity initiatives and their alignment 
with business objectives.
+ Methods: Executive briefings, risk assessment walkthroughs, and real-world case 
studies of cybersecurity incidents.
* General Employees:
+ Focus Areas: Recognizing phishing, malware, and other common cyber threats.
+ Methods: Short e-learning modules, gamified quizzes, and group awareness 
sessions.
Incident Awareness and Response
Employees play a crucial role in identifying and responding to cybersecurity incidents. This 
policy ensures that all personnel are equipped with the knowledge to detect, report, and 
escalate potential threats promptly, minimizing risks to the organization.
Training Content:
* Phishing Attempts: Emails or messages containing suspicious links, requests for sensitive 
information, or unexpected attachments.
* Unauthorized Access Requests: Any attempt to gain access to systems or data without 
proper authorization.
* Unusual Network Activity: Indicators such as slow performance, unexpected system 
behavior, or unknown devices connected to the network.
* System Anomalies: Alerts, crashes, or unauthorized changes to system configurations.
Incident Reporting Procedures:
* Reporting Mechanism: Employees are required to report incidents through the 
organization's designated incident management system (e.g., EnerGuard ™), accessible via 
the internal portal or a hotline.
* Escalation Steps:
+ Initial report is reviewed by the IT Security Team to assess severity and scope.
+ High-priority incidents are escalated to senior cybersecurity personnel and the 
Risk Management Team.
+ Relevant stakeholders, including department heads and vendors, are notified if 
their assets or processes are impacted.
Compliance and Alignment
The training policy adheres to:
* ISO 27001 standards for information security.
* GDPR requirements for data protection.
* NIS2 Directive, Article 21, Clause 2(g), to strengthen cybersecurity capabilities.
* NIST guidelines for cybersecurity awareness and training.
Training Records
To ensure compliance with regulatory standards and facilitate continuous improvement in 
cybersecurity training, the organization maintains comprehensive training records. These 
records include:
* Participant Lists and Attendance Records: Documentation of all personnel, contractors, 
and vendors who participate in training sessions.
* Completion Dates and Assessment Scores: Records of training completion dates and, 
where applicable, individual assessment scores to evaluate the effectiveness of the training 
programs and identify areas for improvement.
* Simulated Phishing Exercises and Outcomes: Detailed logs of simulated exercises (such as 
phishing), including participant performance and overall outcomes, to assess practical 
cybersecurity awareness and identify trends in vulnerability.
Policy Review and Updates
The Cybersecurity Training Policy is reviewed annually or after significant cybersecurity 
incidents to incorporate:
* Emerging threats and new attack vectors.
* Changes in regulatory or industry standards.
* Advancements in training tools and methodologies.
Updates require formal approval by the Compliance Committee and senior management.
